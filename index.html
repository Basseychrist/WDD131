Official Flag of Nigeria

Nigeria is a country located in West Africa, known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse population, and significant economic influence on the continent. It is the most populous country in Africa, with over 200 million inhabitants, comprising various ethnic groups such as the Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo, and many others. The official language is English, reflecting its colonial history. Nigeria's economy is the largest in Africa, largely driven by oil exports, although agriculture, telecommunications, and services are also important sectors. The country boasts a vibrant cultural scene, with globally recognized contributions to music, literature, and film, particularly through Nollywood, one of the largest film industries in the world. The capital city is Abuja, while Lagos, the largest city, serves as the economic hub. Nigeria's landscape ranges from savannas to rainforests, and it is home to notable landmarks such as the Zuma Rock, the Niger River, and numerous national parks. Despite challenges such as political instability and economic disparities, Nigeria continues to play a crucial role in regional and global affairs.
